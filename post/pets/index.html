Pets

My furry friend

Something that equates with my love for physics are cats, I really love them, they are cute, funny, playful, curious, etc. Since I was a kid I’ve loved cats, they are fluffy and adorable, although sometimes are little demons that love the chaos, but who can blame them? For their cuteness everything is forgiven.

I’ve had other pets, actually a lot: birds, fishes, turtles, hamsters (a lot), rabbits, dogs, and chickens, but no one is compared with cats. Ironically, at the beginning I was allergic to them, my face turned really red and it made me itch, I actually don’t know exactly to what was I allergic, but fortunately at the end I overcame it. I remember that one time my brothers and I found three kittens in the park and we took them to the house without our mother knowing about it, because she wouldn’t let us keep them, but after two days with them my face was so red that my mom figured it out and gave them up for adoption, it was really sad and funny.

The first cat we had, that I was conscious because we had some when I was 3, was a Siamese cat named Lulu, it was a birthday present for my brother of one of his friends, so my mom let us keep it. We had it when I was in elementary school, in 5th grade, and after three years with it my mom ended up giving it to an acquaintance. I do not remember how much time happened but one day a kitty appeared outside our house and of course we kept it, it had the same colors as the Siamese but it wasn’t one.

Unlike Lulu, which was somewhat aggressive, this cat was pure love, without doubt the most affectionate cat we’ve had and for a lack of creativity it was also called Lulu. Unfortunately this cat was poisoned when I was at my last year of middle school, we cried a lot. Curiously, one week after that another kitty appeared, one Monday at the morning next to the car when I was just going to go school. I grabbed it and yelled at my mom: “Mom! Look, a kitty!”. It was also a Siamese!

Since I was going to go to school my mom woke my brother to show him the cat, and because I was in a semi-boarding school I couldn’t know what happened until Friday. I was so anxious when that day came and when I arrived to the house I saw the kitty still in there. My mom told us that we wouldn’t keep it (haha, how innocent), but just in case we didn’t give it a name so we wouldn’t get attach to it, we just called it Toncha, a Mexican way to say cat, and since then the name remained.

Toncha is like a combination of personality of the previous two cats, she’s affectionate but after some time she gets aggressive, and is really playful. It’s sometimes frustrating that attitude because she looks adorable so you inevitable want to pet her, but she ends up biting or scratching you. For the time we’ve been with her, recently she’s been less aggressive and sometimes she even looks up for you.

At the time I am writing this, we’ve been with her for 5 years and in October it would be six, so all of us love her so much that when she dies we’ll be devastated, but that’s not something to think about now. We’ve taken her so much pictures along these five years, I just put two of those pictures but you can check up more at my instragram.

Posing her beauty A warm winter

Some funny facts, that I think a lot of cats do (at least from my friends and mine), is that they sleep anywhere, literally anywhere, Toncha sometimes sleeps in a couch, others in our beds, on the floor, on the table, chairs, on top of books, etc., each time she surprises us with a new place to take a nap. The other thing is the food, even when her food dish is full but is at the edges, she starts to meow for food and we have to move it from the edges to the center.

Talking about meows, she barely does it, we don’t know why but is curious. Our mother is happy with it because the previous cats meowed so much that she was sick of it, however it is sometimes something bad because, like many cats, she is very curious, so when we open a closet she gets inside of it without us noticing it and when we closed it she gets trapped in there and because she doesn’t meow we don’t notice it until some hours that she doesn0t appear anywhere.

There a lot of funny stories that we’ve lived with Toncha, and that’s another reason why I love her so much and the cats in general.

Ulises Zarate
